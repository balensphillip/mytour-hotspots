My Bucketlist

Pyramids of Giza, Egypt

The pyramids are massive stone structures that were built in ancient Egypt thousands of years ago. They were tombs for pharaohs, or kings, and their consorts, and were meant to help the pharaohs achieve eternal life in the afterworld.The most famous pyramids are the three Great Pyramids of Giza, which were built during the Old Kingdom period between 2580 and 2560 BCE.

Archipelago, Cape Verde

Cape verde is a small island in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean, It started as a village called Ribeira Grande. The village was located on the island of Santo Antao, which is part of the archipelago of Cape Verde. The people of Ribeira Grande were known for their kindness and hospitality.

Great Wall of China, China

The Great Wall of China was built starting in the 7th century BC and continuing through the Ming Dynasty, which ended in the 17th century. It was constructed to protect China from invaders. It was made from a variety of materials, including earth, stone, and brick. It was built in sections that stretched for thousands of miles, through rugged terrain and over steep mountains.

Sossusvlei, Namibia

Sossusvlei is a famous destination in Namibia known for its massive red sand dunes that tower over the surrounding desert landscape. The dunes were formed over millions of years by the constant movement of sand and wind, and they are some of the highest in the world. Despite the harsh desert conditions, life still manages to thrive in Sossusvlei.

Faruk-tokluoğlu, Morocco

Morocco is home to several breathtaking oases, some of which have been inhabited for centuries. One of the most famous is the Draa Valley oasis, which stretches for nearly 200 km along the Draa River in southeastern Morocco. The Draa Valley oasis is dotted with date palms and small villages, and it is a popular destination for tourists interested in experiencing traditional Moroccan culture and architecture.

Moscow, Russia

Moscow is the capital and largest city of Russia, with a population of over 12 million people. It is one of the most important political, economic, and cultural centers of the country. Moscow has a rich history with iconic landmarks like Red Square, and St. Basil's Cathedral. The city has an extensive metro system and well decorated intricate mosaics, statues and frescoes.

Machu Picchu, Peru

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It was built in the 15th century and its purpose remains a mystery. The city was abandoned in the 16th century during the Spanish conquest of South America and was eventually forgotten by the outside world. In 1911, an American explorer named Hiram Bingham stumbled upon the city while searching for Vilcabamba, the last stronghold of the Incas.

Grand Canyon, United States of America

The Grand Canyon is a massive geological wonder located in Arizona, USA. It was formed over millions of years by the Colorado River cutting through the rock, exposing layers of sediment and creating a breathtaking natural masterpiece.The canyon is over 277 miles long, up to 18 miles wide, and up to a mile deep.

Colosseum, Italy

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient structure located in Rome, Italy that was built nearly 2,000 years ago. Construction of the Colosseum began in 72 AD under the reign of Emperor Vespasian and was completed in 80 AD under the reign of his son, Titus.

Al Aqsa Mosque, Jerusalem

The Al-Aqsa Mosque is located in the Old City of Jerusalem and is one of the most important Islamic holy sites in the world. The mosque is believed to have been built in the early 7th century, just a few years after the Prophet Muhammad's death. It is the third holiest site in Islam after the cities of Mecca and Medina.
